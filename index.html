How it works

Polygon Staking in three steps

Use the app to prepare a staking transaction, inspect the live terms, and confirm only from your wallet. The flow keeps you close to the chain instead of burying the important details.

Step 01

Connect your wallet

Open the app with a compatible self-custody wallet. Your keys stay with you, and every approval is visible before anything moves.

Step 02

Choose POL and a validator

Select the POL amount, review validator information, and check the live staking terms shown in the interface. The app keeps reward context and network cost details in the same place.

Step 03

Confirm and track

Sign the transaction only after the preview looks right. Once confirmed, use the app and the network explorer to follow status, rewards, and unstaking actions.

Why stake

FAQ

Polygon Staking questions

01

What is Polygon Staking?

Polygon Staking is the process of using POL in a staking flow connected to Polygon validators. The app helps you prepare the transaction, review live details, and sign from your own wallet.

02

Which asset do I stake?

The app is designed around POL, Polygon's current ecosystem token. If a wallet or venue still shows legacy naming, confirm the token and network in the app before signing.

03

How are rewards generated?

Rewards come from validator participation in proof-of-stake consensus and the live staking terms displayed in the app. The page does not publish fixed APRs because those values move.

04

Is the app non-custodial?

Yes. You connect a wallet, review the transaction, and sign yourself. The app is a staking interface, not an exchange account or a custodian for your private keys.

05

What does it cost?

You pay the network costs required for the on-chain actions you choose, such as staking, approvals, or unstaking. The app shows the current cost preview before confirmation.

06

Which chains are supported?

Supported networks depend on the active staking route and wallet connection. See the full list on the supported chains page.
